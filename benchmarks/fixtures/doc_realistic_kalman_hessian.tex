\section{Kalman likelihood Hessian}
The innovation contribution to the likelihood depends on the residual and its covariance.
A Hessian implementation has to account for derivatives of both the covariance inverse and the residual.

\begin{equation}\label{eq:kalman-innovation-score-local}
\partial_i \ell_t
= -\frac{1}{2}\operatorname{tr}(S_t^{-1}\partial_i S_t)
+ \frac{1}{2}v_t' S_t^{-1}(\partial_i S_t)S_t^{-1}v_t
- (\partial_i v_t)'S_t^{-1}v_t.
\end{equation}

This local score formula fixes the terms that any second-derivative step must differentiate, but it does not by itself prove a full Hessian block.
