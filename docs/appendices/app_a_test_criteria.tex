\chapter{Detailed Test Criteria}

This appendix provides explicit test criteria for the first major evaluation domains. The intent is to make MathDevMCP measurable and falsifiable.

\section{Monograph support tests}

\begin{longtable}{p{0.22\textwidth}p{0.28\textwidth}p{0.42\textwidth}}
\toprule
\textbf{Test area} & \textbf{Task} & \textbf{Pass criteria} \\
\midrule
Retrieval & Find the exact proposition, definition, equation, or appendix entry for a natural-language query & Correct file and local label found with high top-$k$ recall and low hallucinated references \\
Notation consistency & Update a section that reuses notation from distant chapters & No introduced symbol drift, inconsistent reuse, or broken cross-reference \\
Draft grounding & Draft a section from indexed local sources & Claims are linked to retrieved evidence and unsupported additions are flagged \\
Revision support & Apply a change to notation or assumptions and propagate consequences & Affected chapters and references are correctly identified and revision checklist is generated \\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}

\section{Code--document consistency tests}

\begin{longtable}{p{0.22\textwidth}p{0.28\textwidth}p{0.42\textwidth}}
\toprule
\textbf{Test area} & \textbf{Task} & \textbf{Pass criteria} \\
\midrule
Equation--code alignment & Compare a documented formula with its implementation & Correctly identify match or mismatch and localize the relevant code path \\
Assumption consistency & Check whether stated assumptions are enforced or relied upon in code & Correct trace from assumption to implementation/test artifact \\
Algorithm drift & Detect when text describes an obsolete algorithm variant & Seeded drift is detected with high precision and actionable localization \\
Dimension consistency & Compare documented dimensions with runtime structures & Shape mismatch or agreement is stated correctly and supported by evidence \\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}

\section{Code generation tests}

\begin{longtable}{p{0.22\textwidth}p{0.28\textwidth}p{0.42\textwidth}}
\toprule
\textbf{Test area} & \textbf{Task} & \textbf{Pass criteria} \\
\midrule
Specification fidelity & Generate code from a mathematical specification & Generated code preserves the required structure, variables, and conventions \\
Executable correctness & Run unit and scenario checks derived from the specification & Generated code passes acceptance checks without hidden manual repair \\
Traceability & Explain where the generated code came from & Output is tied back to equations, assumptions, and source snippets \\
Hallucination control & Inspect for invented routines or unsupported simplifications & Low hallucination rate relative to baseline generation workflows \\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}

\section{Documentation auditing tests}

\begin{longtable}{p{0.22\textwidth}p{0.28\textwidth}p{0.42\textwidth}}
\toprule
\textbf{Test area} & \textbf{Task} & \textbf{Pass criteria} \\
\midrule
Verdict accuracy & Classify a claim as correct, incorrect, gapful, ambiguous, or unverifiable & Classification matches gold or reviewer consensus \\
Gap localization & Identify the broken step or missing assumption & Localized finding points to the right proof segment, equation, or code path \\
Counterexample generation & Produce a contradiction when a claim is false & Counterexample is valid, relevant, and reproducible \\
Unsupported claim detection & Flag claims not justified by local evidence & Reduced rate of confident unsupported conclusions \\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}

\section{Team adoption tests}

\begin{itemize}
  \item A new teammate can use a documented workflow without bespoke coaching.
  \item The same task produces similar outputs across users because tools and reports are structured.
  \item Time-to-first-draft and time-to-reviewed-draft decrease on representative tasks.
  \item Failure modes are understandable and do not silently masquerade as success.
\end{itemize}
