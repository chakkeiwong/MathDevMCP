# MathDevMCP Academic Governance Reset

Date: 2026-07-13

Status: `ACTIVE_PROSPECTIVE_GOVERNANCE`

## Decision

MathDevMCP is an academic research and scientific-software repository. Its
governance must be proportional to the risk of the work. The cryptographic
execution state machine introduced for the real-document remediation program
is deprecated prospectively because it consumed substantial effort without
materially strengthening mathematical, numerical, or scientific evidence.

The governing principle is:

> Explore broadly, test seriously, and be rigorous at the claim boundary.

Existing P00-P03 receipts, indexes, reviews, digests, blockers, and recovery
plans remain historical evidence. They are not deleted or rewritten. They no
longer constitute live execution authority, and a failed legacy receipt does
not prevent retrying an ordinary engineering operation under this reset.

## What Remains Strict

The reset does not weaken these boundaries:

- mathematical claims require a derivation in project notation, an adequate
  technical citation, or verification by an appropriate certifying backend;
- assumptions, source scope, provenance, and unresolved uncertainty must
  remain explicit;
- retrieval, lexical matches, generated derivations, CAS simplifications, and
  proof-state traces are not proof by themselves;
- meaningful experiments require a predeclared evidence contract, appropriate
  comparators, veto diagnostics, run provenance, and a result note;
- source-document edits, publication enablement, release/default changes,
  destructive operations, credentials, and external disclosure retain their
  human and security boundaries;
- unrelated dirty work remains protected;
- repair publication remains disabled until the master program's scientific
  promotion conditions are actually satisfied.

## What Is Deprecated

The following mechanisms are no longer required for routine phase execution:

- append-only command receipts and receipt-index chains;
- hashing every plan, review, log, command, and handoff;
- one-shot local commands that cannot be retried after an engineering error;
- exact review-label grammars;
- review-budget authorization and consumption ledgers;
- recovery subplans for ordinary controller, test, formatting, or bookkeeping
  defects;
- independent review of clerical changes;
- stable hard-link publication as a prerequisite for starting later academic
  development.

These mechanisms may be reintroduced only for a concrete security, external
deployment, irreversible publication, or release-integrity risk. Their use
must name that risk and explain why normal git history, tests, and scientific
artifacts are insufficient.

## Proportional Risk Tiers

| Tier | Typical work | Required control |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1: routine engineering | Implementation, refactoring, debugging, parser fixtures, local diagnostics | Preserve dirty work; inspect the diff; run focused tests and relevant adjacent tests. Retry ordinary failures after diagnosis. |
| 2: scientific or numerical decision | Experiments, benchmarks, method comparisons, search-budget/default choices, interpretation of real-document behavior | Skeptical plan audit; default/assumption audit; evidence contract; exact command/environment/seeds when applicable; result note with veto-first interpretation and non-claims. |
| 3: mathematical or product claim | Claimed proof/refutation, promoted repair, publication enablement, default-policy or release decision | Tier 2 plus claim-scoped evidence, appropriate external/certifying tools, reproducible artifacts, and one substantive independent review. Human approval remains required for publication/default/release boundaries. |
| 4: security or irreversible action | Credentials, private-data disclosure, destructive operations, remote publication, deployment | Explicit authorization and the applicable security controls. |

Test counts, reviewer agreement, artifact volume, and digest-chain completeness
are explanatory in every tier. They cannot override a failed scientific or
mathematical veto.

## Lean Phase Protocol

For Phases 03-09 of the real-document remediation program:

1. Read the master phase objective and audit the proposed work skeptically.
2. State the smallest evidence contract that answers the phase question.
3. Implement only the necessary scope.
4. Run focused checks, then relevant adjacent checks in proportion to risk.
5. Write one concise phase result containing the decision, evidence, residual
   risks, non-claims, and next justified phase.
6. Obtain one substantive review when the phase changes a claim boundary,
   scientific interpretation, important default, or publication/release
   decision. Ordinary repairs after a concrete review finding need focused
   verification, not repeated reviews of bookkeeping.
7. Advance when the phase's scientific/engineering criteria pass and vetoes
   are clear. A legacy governance artifact is not an advancement criterion.

## Automatic Phase Loop

Prospectively, the real-document remediation program runs continuously through
ready local phases. At the end of each subplan, the supervisor must:

1. run the required focused and adjacent local checks;
2. write or refresh the phase result/close record with evidence, vetoes,
   residual risks, and non-claims;
3. draft or refresh the next phase subplan from the actual predecessor state;
4. skeptically review the next subplan for baseline correctness, assumptions,
   feasibility, artifact coverage, stop conditions, and boundary safety;
5. launch the next phase automatically when that review passes and its entry
   conditions are met.

A fresh independent reviewer may add useful criticism, but ordinary local
phase launch does not wait indefinitely for an advisory reviewer. Silence,
timeout, or reviewer unavailability is recorded and cannot count as agreement;
it also does not block a locally audited Tier 1 or Tier 2 phase. One substantive
independent review remains mandatory before closing a phase that changes a
mathematical/product claim boundary, important scientific interpretation,
default, publication, or release decision.

The loop pauses and asks for help only when the unresolved choice materially
changes scientific interpretation or project direction, crosses publication,
default, release, privacy, permission, cost, destructive, or irreversible
boundaries, requires an unapproved external action, or remains genuinely
unresolved after safe local investigation and repair. Ordinary implementation
defects, failing tests, stale prose, and retryable local commands enter the
repair loop rather than stopping the program.

Plans remain required for long or research-decision-making runs under the
repository scientific policy. A plan is not required merely because a local
test or generator may fail and need a retry.

## Digest Policy

Use content digests when identity matters to the scientific question:

- frozen source documents and data;
- extracted obligations and exact source spans;
- backend-native inputs and results;
- evidence manifests supporting a promoted repair;
- fixed experimental baselines when silent substitution would invalidate a
  comparison.

Do not require digests for ordinary prose plans, review wording, test logs, or
local command transitions unless a specific claim depends on their exact
bytes.

- P01 evidence identity/integrity remains retained implementation and test
  evidence.
- P02 label-scoped extraction remains retained implementation and regression
  evidence.
- Formal P03 `rr01` remains historically rejected because its search-boundary
  mutation was inadequate and a falsified budget was accepted.
- The P03 defect has since been repaired in
  `src/mathdevmcp/context_evidence.py` with focused regressions in
  `tests/test_context_evidence.py`.
- The failed P03 `close_round` receipt and P03R2 recovery reviews remain useful
  diagnostics of the deprecated protocol. The P03R2 recovery path must not be
  executed under this reset.

contract. If that contract passes, a concise academic result may close P03 and
open P04 planning while publication remains disabled.

## Stop Conditions

Stop for user direction only when the remaining choice materially changes
scientific interpretation, project direction, cost, privacy, permissions,
irreversible state, publication, default behavior, or release claims. Ordinary
test failures, fixable code defects, stale documentation, and retryable local
commands are not human stop conditions.

## Non-Claims

This reset is not evidence of mathematical correctness, scientific validity,
backend fitness, publication readiness, or release readiness. It changes the
cost and proportionality of process while preserving the standards applied to
actual scientific and mathematical claims.
