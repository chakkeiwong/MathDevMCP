## External-Tool Ledger

Phase 03 required deterministic source traversal and provenance validation, not
mathematical derivation. The repository-native byte-preserving locator and
entry-rooted context graph were used. SymPy, SageMath, Lean, LeanSearch-v2,
LeanExplore, jixia, Pantograph, and LeanDojo were considered but not invoked:
there was no formal claim, algebraic identity, proof state, or library premise
query for them to certify in this phase. Their absence from this result is not
evidence against any mathematical claim.

## Post-Run Red Team

The strongest alternative explanation is corpus overfitting: exact behavior
on the reviewed fixtures and two frozen documents does not imply robust
context classification for macro-generated includes, external build roots, or
unreviewed LaTeX conventions. Such cases must remain `not_searched` or explicit
engineering diagnostics until supported.

The weakest scientific evidence is the meaning of `source_supported`. It proves
only that an exact, applicable source node was found under the current
classifier. It does not show the assumption is true, sufficient, correctly
formalized, or usable by a backend.

This conclusion would be overturned by a reproducible case where an omitted
graph boundary survives validation, an irrelevant candidate becomes explicit
support, `\pi` becomes posterior from spelling, an extraction veto traverses
semantics, or a context result enables publication/backend claims.
